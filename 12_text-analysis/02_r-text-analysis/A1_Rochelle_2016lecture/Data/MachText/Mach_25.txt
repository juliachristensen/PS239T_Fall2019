IV: Why the Kingdom of Darius, conquered by Alexander, did not rebel against his successors after Alexander's death  Given the difficulties encountered in holding a newly conquered territory, the following facts may cause surprise. Alexander the Great became master of Asia within a few years and, not long afterwards, died. It might have been expected that the whole region would then rebel; nevertheless, Alexander's successors held it, and had to contend with no other difficulties than those arising from their own ambitious schemes.
