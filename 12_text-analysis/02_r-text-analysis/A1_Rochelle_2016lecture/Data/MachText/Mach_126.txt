Everywhere the common people are the vast majority, and the few are isolated when the majority and the government are at one. One present- day ruler, whom it is well to leave unnamed, is always preaching peace and trust, although he is really very hostile to both; and if he had practised them he would have lost either reputation or power several times over.  XIX: How contempt and hatred should be avoided  Since I have already discussed the most important of the qualities previously mentioned, I want to discuss the others briefly under this general heading: that a ruler, as has already been partly explained, should avoid anything that will make him either hated or despised.
