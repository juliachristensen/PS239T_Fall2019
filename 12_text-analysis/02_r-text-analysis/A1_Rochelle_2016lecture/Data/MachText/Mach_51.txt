 But let me continue from where I left off. I say that the Duke was very powerful, and secure in some measure against existing dangers, because he possessed his own troops, and had largely destroyed those neighbouring forces that could have harmed him. Since he wanted to annex more territory, he had to be very careful about the King of France. For he recognised that the King, who had belatedly realised his mistake, would not tolerate this plan. The Duke therefore began to seek new alliances, and to temporise with the King of France when the French undertook a campaign in the Kingdom of Naples against the Spaniards, who were besieging Gaeta. His aim was to protect himself against them; if Alexander had not died, he would soon have succeeded.  This was how he acted in relation to the existing situation.
