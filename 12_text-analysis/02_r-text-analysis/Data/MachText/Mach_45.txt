Apart from this problem, Alexander saw that the Italian military forces (and especially those that he could have used most easily) belonged to those who had every reason to fear any increase of the Pope's power; therefore, he could not safely use them, because they all belonged to the Orsini and Colonna factions and their adherents.  It was therefore necessary to sow disorder in Italy, making their states unstable, so as to be able to seize and hold some portion of them. This was easy for him, because he found that the Venetians, for other reasons, wanted to bring the French back into Italy. Not only did he not oppose this policy; he facilitated it by annulling the first marriage of King Louis. The King, then, invaded Italy with the help of the Venetians and the consent of Alexander.
